Our Story

Born from lived experience.
Built with purpose.

Rise Again was founded by Josephine Duba Kanu, a Sierra Leonean youth advocate who grew up in Kalangba Town, Bombali District. Growing up, Josephine witnessed many young girls whose dreams ended the moment they became mothers not because of their circumstances, but because of how the world responded to those circumstances.


Instead of support, these girls faced stigma, rejection, and isolation. Instead of opportunity, they faced closed doors. Rise Again exists to open those doors back up through education, skills, mentorship, and advocacy.


Inspired by the Girls in Diplomacy Fellowship and grounded in community experience, Rise Again is not a charity. It is a movement built on the belief that dignity, opportunity, and leadership belong to every young woman, regardless of her story.

"Becoming a teenage mother should never mean the end of someone's future. Everyone deserves a second chance. That belief gave birth to Rise Again."
Josephine Duba Kanu, Founder
The Problem

The barriers teenage mothers face

In Sierra Leone, teenage mothers face compounding challenges that trap them in cycles of poverty and exclusion. Rise Again addresses each one.

Dropping out of school

Most teenage mothers are forced to leave school losing their education and their future opportunities.

Stigma and rejection

Communities, families, and institutions often respond with judgment rather than support.

Financial hardship

Without education or skills, young mothers have few paths to financial independence.

Mental health struggles

Isolation, shame, and lack of support take a severe toll on young mothers' wellbeing.

Closed doors

Leadership, decision-making, and opportunity spaces remain largely inaccessible to young mothers.

Policy gaps

Policies that should protect young mothers are absent, weak, or unenforced across Sierra Leone.

What We Do

Five pillars of empowerment

Rise Again takes a holistic approach because one intervention alone is not enough to break cycles that run this deep.

01
Education Support

We advocate for re-enrollment of teenage mothers in schools, connect girls with scholarships and learning opportunities, and promote flexible education pathways that work around the realities of young motherhood.

Back to school
02
Skills Development

Vocational training in tailoring, hairdressing, ICT and digital skills, entrepreneurship, agriculture, and small business management building pathways to financial independence.

Economic freedom
03
Leadership Development

Training teenage mothers to become community leaders, youth advocates, public speakers, peace ambassadors, and mentors for other young women.

Lead the way
04
Mental Health & Wellbeing

Counselling, peer support groups, confidence-building workshops, and emotional healing spaces because healing is not optional, it is foundational.

Heal and grow
05
Policy Advocacy

Working with schools, communities, government institutions, NGOs, and international organisations to promote policies that protect teenage mothers and reduce stigma across Sierra Leone and beyond.

Systemic change
Our Vision

A Sierra Leone where every teenage mother has the opportunity to rebuild her life.

Rise Again is more than a project it is a movement. We are working toward a future where no young woman is defined by her circumstances, but empowered by her potential.

Josephine Duba Kanu is a Sierra Leonean youth advocate, software engineer, and social entrepreneur currently studying at African Leadership University in Kigali, Rwanda. She grew up in Kalangba Town, Bombali District a rural community where she witnessed firsthand the consequences of inequality for women and girls.

As a UN Millennium Fellow, she conducted research on maternal health challenges in rural Sierra Leone and built SAFEBIRTH Sierra Leone an AI-powered maternal health early warning system. Her Girls in Diplomacy Fellowship deepened her understanding of human rights, feminist foreign policy, and how to design systemic change rather than temporary relief.

Rise Again brings together both passions: technology and humanity. It is Josephine's commitment that no girl in Sierra Leone should lose her future because the world responded to her circumstances with judgment instead of support.
